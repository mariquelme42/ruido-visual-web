Design a desktop website UI for an independent collective editorial project called "Ruido Visual".

Style reference:
Use the visual style of https://enigma.mx/ as inspiration:
– minimal but experimental
– editorial typography
– strong use of whitespace
– poetic and conceptual layout
– contemporary art / cultural institution aesthetic
– mix of serif and sans-serif fonts
– calm, intellectual, artistic tone
– not corporate, not startup style
– more like a cultural archive / art collective / experimental editorial

Concept:
Ruido Visual is an independent, self-managed, collective editorial network of writers and artists.
The website should feel like:
– a poetic archive
– a collective laboratory
– a digital editorial space
– a cultural platform
– a living library

Tone of text:
Use poetic language inspired by this concept:

"Ruido Visual is a collective, independent, self-managed editorial network born from a poetic residency.  
It exists as a space for shared creation, mutual support, and collective publication.  
The project seeks to open paths for writers who do not find space in traditional publishing structures.  
The editorial process is collaborative, horizontal, and sustained by trust, friendship, and artistic commitment.  
The website should feel like an archive of voices, a space of memory, and a place for future works."

Do NOT use corporate tone.
Use poetic, reflective, artistic language.

Structure of the website:

Create multiple pages / screens:

1. HOME
Sections:
– Header with logo and menu
– Hero with title "Ruido Visual"
– Short poetic description
– Featured library section
– Featured gallery section
– Open calls / convocatorias
– Footer

2. ABOUT
Sections:
– What is Ruido Visual
– Collective editorial philosophy
– Principles: collaboration, respect, collective creation, freedom of expression
– Text blocks with poetic tone
– Clean editorial layout

3. LIBRARY
A digital archive of publications

Show:
– grid of books / publications
– each item has cover, title, author, year
– buttons: Read online / Download PDF

Filters UI:
– year
– author
– type
– collection

Style like an art archive, not a bookstore.

4. PUBLICATION PAGE
Layout:
– book cover on left
– metadata on right
– title, author, year, description
– buttons: Read / Download
– text reading section below
– related publications

5. GALLERY
Visual grid layout
Large images
Art documentation style

6. CALLS / CONVOCATORIAS
Show open calls for:
– residencies
– publications
– collaborations

Include:
– text description
– download rules
– form UI
– file upload field
– poetic but clear layout

Images:
Use conceptual photography style.
Use images similar to:
– hands holding printed cards
– nature textures
– street signs
– analog photography
– printed paper
– editorial objects

Color palette:
– off white
– black
– dark green
– muted tones
– paper-like textures
– subtle grain

Typography:
Mix serif + sans serif
Editorial style

Important:
Design must feel like an art collective website,
not a startup,
not a business,
not a blog,
but a cultural archive / experimental editorial platform.

Make the layout modern but human.
Make it poetic, minimal, and structured.
